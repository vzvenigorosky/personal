About

I am a forensic geneticist and molecular biologist based in Strasbourg, working at the intersection of the laboratory and the computer. My research uses high-throughput sequencing and biostatistics to answer questions in forensic identification, population history and disease — from recovering the genome of Treponema pallidum (the syphilis bacterium) out of decades-old archived tissue, to reconstructing kinship and migration across ancient Eurasia.

Over the past decade I have taken part in fifteen years of Franco-Yakut expeditions in North-Eastern Siberia, published across forensic, evolutionary and archaeological genetics, taught molecular biology and forensic kinship to Master's students, and built and run an ISO 17025 quality-management system for a forensic genetics laboratory. I care about turning complex genetic data into clear narratives — and about teaching the next generation to do the same.
