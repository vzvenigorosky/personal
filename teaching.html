Teaching

I teach molecular biology, forensic identification and population genetics to Master's students, and I design and grade the associated examinations. Two courses have made up the bulk of my teaching.

Forensic genetic identification & genetic kinship

Master's in Forensic Medicine & Criminalistics · years 1 & 2

~210 hours

Nine weeks (about 240 hours, eight of them delivered online) covering the methods of molecular biology — in particular the use of forensic STR markers — the genetic profiling and identification of individuals, and the statistical methods of kinship determination using Bayesian models.

I prepared and graded both the first- and second-year examinations for the past three years.

Molecular biology Forensic STR markers Genetic profiling Bayesian kinship models

Population genetics

Master's year 1 in Ecology, Evolution & Biology Education · University of Strasbourg

16 hours

Five lessons (16 hours total) at the Faculty of Sciences of the University of Strasbourg, focused on the use and interpretation of statistical methods and graphics in population genetics.

I prepared two examination questions each year I taught the course and graded them.

Population genetics Statistical methods Data visualisation

Guest lectures & field seminars

I have also given lectures on genetic identification in archaeological contexts at the University of Yakutsk and during Franco-Yakut field seminars. These appear alongside my conference communications on the Talks page.
